About Us

Welcome to our poultry farm! We are a family-owned and operated business that has been providing high-quality poultry products to our customers for many years. We are passionate about what we do, and we take great pride in the quality of our products.

At our farm, we believe in sustainable and humane farming practices.

We raise our poultry in a free-range environment, where they have ample space to roam and access to fresh air and sunlight. Our birds are fed a natural and balanced diet, which ensures that they grow healthy and strong.

Our team comprises experienced farmers and animal husbandry experts who are dedicated to ensuring the welfare of our birds. We take a hands-on approach to farming, and we are involved in every step of the process, from raising and feeding our birds to processing and packaging our products.

We are committed to providing our customers with the highest quality poultry products. We believe in transparency and honesty, and we are always happy to answer any questions you may have about our farming practices or our products.

  • Our goal is to provide our customers with the freshest and most delicious poultry products available.
  • We take great pride in our farm and the products we produce.
  • we are committed to providing our customers with exceptional service and quality products.

We look forward to serving you and sharing our passion for farming and high-quality poultry products with you.

Frequently Asked Questions

A poultry farm is a facility where domesticated birds such as chickens, ducks, geese, and turkeys are raised for their eggs or meat. These farms are usually large-scale operations that are designed to maximize production efficiency and ensure the health and well-being of the birds.

Poultry farming is the form of animal husbandry which raises domesticated birds such as chickens, ducks, turkeys and geese to produce meat or eggs for food.

Layer poultry farming means raising egg laying poultry birds for the purpose of commercial egg production.

They are raised in large, open structures known as grow out houses. A farmer receives the birds from the hatchery at one day old.A grow out consists of 5 to 9 weeks according to how big the kill plant wants the chickens to be. These houses are equipped with mechanical systems to deliver feed and water to the birds.

Chicken lifespans vary widely, with most hens generally living between 3 and 7 years. However, with ideal care, they may live even longer.

While Leghorns come in many recognized varieties, the white Leghorn is by far the most popular and is the best egg laying variety. White leghorns are a standard breed used by the commercial egg industry for producing white eggs.
